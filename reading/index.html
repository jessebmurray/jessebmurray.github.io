What I'm Reading

Climate Change and Humanity's Dependence on Ammonia Fertilizer • Dec 2019

In this excellent article, Leigh Boerner describes how the Haber-Bosch process to produce ammonia fertilizer, which about 50% of the world's food production depends on, accounts for slightly more than 1% of global annual CO2 emissions. The Haber-Bosch process releases emissions for two reasons: The first is to supply the energy needed to run the reaction, which occurs at temperatures around 500 ºC and pressures around 200 atmospheres. The second is to produce the hydrogen used to turn N2 from the atmosphere into to NH3 fertilizer, which primarily uses natural gas, but also uses coal or oil. The article discusses a number of initiatives by researchers to develop new ways of producing ammonia fertilizer that don't emit CO2.
