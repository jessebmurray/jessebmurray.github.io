About


I am a graduate student at the University of Oxford pursuing computational biology. Currently, I am studying the MSc in Statistical Science - a twelve-month Master's degree that teaches statistics and machine learning, and involves a research dissertation. Afterwards, I will stay at Oxford to be a PhD candidate at the SABS R3 Centre for Doctoral Training. I am also continuing research from a summer internship at NASA Ames Research Center on the statistical classification of biosignatures for life-detection missions.

After high school, I spent a year at Juilliard studying the cello. However, my interests became too broad and academic for a career in music. I then transferred late in the summer to Drew University where I studied as a physics major and mathematics minor. Initially aiming for a medical career, I gradually discovered my passion for mathematical modeling and gaining insights from large, complex datasets.

My undergraduate research was on the (largely unconscious) cognitive process that occurs when solving an anagram puzzle. I designed an original eye-tracking experiment, and from the coordinate-time-series data on 29 participants I was able to obtain new results that I later presented at a neuroscience conference.

My hobbies include small research projects that apply modeling and computational approaches to real-world situations. I also make occasional contributions to Wikipedia under the username MurrayScience and I enjoy visiting nature preserves.
