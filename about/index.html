About



In the fall of 2020, I will begin my studies at Columbia Univeristy for a data science MS. In college, I was a physics major and math minor, and I got into coding and data science through side projects and eye-tracking research. When I’m not doing research or taking classes, I enjoy thinking of interesting questions to answer with a math and coding involved project. If it’s a good question, then I enjoy working very hard and learning new skills to try to answer it.

After graduating from high school, I attended Juilliard for a year to study cello. Although I enjoyed music, I realized that my interests were much broader and more academic. In late summer, I transferred to Drew University to study physics. Initially, I wanted to go into medicine, however, I discovered that I loved working in math, data, and computer science much more. One of my biggest inspirations in this has been my brother. I’m currently learning about statistical and machine learning and working these techniques into new projects.

My hobbies include biking and running in nature preserves, playing Catan, and reading articles.
