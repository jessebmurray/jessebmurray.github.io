Projects

The projects are listed in order of most to least cool (in my opinion). I'll be updating the current projects as I continue to develop them, and adding new projects too.

  • Modeling the growth of landfills over time

    · Updated Dec 2019
    I use data on decomposition rates, landfill depth and density, and annual additions of the different types of waste into landfills to model the growth of landfills into the future. The results show how large we can expect landfills to be in the future and indicate the somewhat surprising waste-type culprits that drive the long-term growth of landfills.

  • The passing on of polygenic traits from parent to offspring populations

    · Updated Dec 2019
    I use concepts such as regression towards the mean, the breeder's equation, and the normal distribution of polygenic traits to simulate what happens to the distribution of a polygenic trait when a 'parent' generation reproduces to create the next 'offspring' generation. The original motivation for this project was to answer the question: Consider the tallest one fifth of trees in a forest. Are more of them the offspring of the previous generation's tallest one fifth or shorter four fifths? While tall trees are more likely to have tall offspring, there are way more average trees than tall trees. This project simulates reproducing polygenic distributions to precisely answer this question and many others.

  • Time travel with special relativity (general coming soon)

    · Updated Dec 2019
    I use equations from special relativity to demonstrate all the amazing things that happen if one chooses to time travel in the only ways we've discovered how: either go very fast or get next to something heavy. These include apparently gaining weight, watching the earth shrink to the width of a pancake, being able to see radio waves, and more. These effects are calculated for you exactly in the interactive code when you decide your date of departure, date of arrival (in the future), and how fast you want to get there. But caution, once you travel to the future, you can't come back!

  • An analysis of the returns from leveraged index funds since 1985

    · Updated Dec 2019
    I use historical data on three different index funds: The NASDAQ Composite, the S&P 500, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average to demonstrate historical returns with annual rebalancing and various rates of leveraging.
