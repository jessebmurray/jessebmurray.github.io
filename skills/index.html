Skills

Research

I am working with Minjoon Kouh on a project about eye-tracking while solving anagrams. From observational experiments, we determined that it could potentially be valuable to accurately know the sequence of letters people read before reaching a solution. Therefore, we designed and ran a highly stimulus-manipulated anagram experiment specifically to this end. From the experimental data, I have obtained some interesting results. As an example, statistics on the number of letters people read before coming up with a solution as a function of the anagram word length. Recently, I have worked on preparing the data for deep learning methods and other prediction models. We would like to use features of the letter sequence data to predict the average performance of that participant and whether the word was solved or not solved. I have gained many valuable skills from this project, including having the opportunity to mentor talented local high school students such as Adwik Rahematpura. I’ll be presenting the results of the research at the University of Scranton Brain and Behavior Conference on March 7th. You can track our progress on this github repository.
